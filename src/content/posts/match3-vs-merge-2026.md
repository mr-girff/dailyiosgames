**TL;DR** — In our current US App Store cohort, match-3 is still the single largest archetype by a wide margin, but merge games punch above their weight on rating and session flexibility. If you want a five-minute filler, match-3 wins. If you want something that survives a two-week commute without an energy wall, merge is the better bet — with caveats.

## The two loops, honestly

Match-3 and merge look like cousins, and they share a monetization playbook, but the core loop rewards different brains.

- **Match-3** is *pattern recognition under a move budget*. You get a board, a move counter, and a goal. The tension is scarcity — every swap counts, and the failure state (out of moves, buy more) is exactly where the paywall lives.
- **Merge** is *spatial compounding*. You combine two of a thing into one better thing, and progress feels like a snowball rather than a gauntlet. The tension is board space, and the paywall lives in *waiting* (timers, energy) rather than *losing*.

That difference matters more than the genre tags suggest. Match-3 sells you out of a fail state. Merge sells you out of a wait state. Both are engineered, but the wait-state model is easier to walk away from without feeling robbed.

## What the data says

Across the games we track daily, match-3 is by far the most crowded shelf — nearly a third of the active cohort on some days. That saturation is a double-edged sword: the top titles (Candy Crush Saga, Royal Match, Block Blast) are polished to a mirror shine, but the long tail is a sea of near-identical clones with reskinned assets and identical IAP tables.

Merge is a smaller shelf, which paradoxically makes it easier to find something good. Fewer entries, higher average ratings, and the mechanic photographs well in a 60-second session or a 30-minute one.

## Who should install which

- **Install a match-3** if you want a game that's *finishable in bursts*, you don't mind a hard energy/moves wall, and you have zero intention of spending. Treat the paywall as the end of today's session, not a purchase prompt.
- **Install a merge** if you want *steady, low-stress progression*, you play in unpredictable chunks, and you value a game you can put down mid-board without penalty.

## The monetization footnote

Both archetypes skew heavily toward the free-with-ads-and-IAP model. In our dataset the overwhelming majority of match-3 and merge titles are `iapAds`. If that's a dealbreaker, the honest move is to skip both and browse our [no ads, no IAP list](/no-ads-no-iap/) — puzzle games that charge once and then leave you alone are rare, but they exist.
